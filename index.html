Skill Exchange

As part of our Human-Computer Interaction course, my partner Jay and I were tasked with designing a UI/UX project focused on solving a real-world problem through effective interface design. We conceptualized and developed SkillExchange, a mobile application that encourages college students to interact, collaborate, and share knowledge and skills in a dynamic, community-driven environment.

The goal of SkillExchange is to create a platform where students can both offer and seek out help in areas like academics, hobbies, and personal growth — essentially creating a give-and-take system. For example, a student proficient in video editing can teach others while learning photography from someone else. We carefully crafted the user flow, wireframes, and interface with user empathy and accessibility in mind.

CodeX

For our final project in Software Engineering, my teammates and I took on the challenge of creating something original and impactful in the field of IoT and software development tools. While exploring existing platforms, we noticed recurring issues—such as heavy interfaces, limited collaboration features, and a lack of student-focused functionalities. From this realization, we conceptualized CodeX, a lightweight, collaborative coding platform designed specifically for students.

Our vision was to develop a tool that not only supports real-time collaboration but also includes modern features tailored to software development learning, such as syntax assistance, version control basics, and easy project sharing. While CodeX is currently documented as a research paper and a Software Requirements Specification (SRS), we're excited about its potential. Now that we're diving into our electives in Game Development, we're planning to build on this foundation and turn CodeX into a fully functional platform in the near future.

Forty31 Coffee Website

For our final project in Information Management, my team and I were assigned to develop a database-driven PHP website based on a company of our choice. We unanimously decided on Forty31, a cozy local coffee shop near our dorm that had become a special place for us — a go-to spot for endless bonding sessions after classes, late-night study breaks, and occasional caffeine-fueled brainstorming.

Our project aimed to create a simple yet functional website for Forty31, complete with features like menu management, inventory tracking, and customer order records, backed by a MySQL database. We focused on building a clean interface and ensuring data was efficiently stored, retrieved, and managed through CRUD operations.
